Project: Investigate a Dataset - [No Show Appointment]¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row.

'PatientId' Unique Identifier for each patient in the dataset 'AppointmentId' appointment identification for each patient ‘ScheduledDay’ tells us on what day the patient set up their appointment. ‘Neighborhood’ indicates the location of the hospital. ‘Scholarship’ indicates whether or not the patient is enrolled in Brasilian welfare program Bolsa Família. 'No-Show'indicates whether or not the patient shows up or not ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

The dataset will be analyzed to determine what factors are important to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

Questions for Analysis¶

(1) What is the gender distribbution of patients who missed their appointments?

(2) Was there a relationship between the age distribution of the patients and their tendency to show up?

(3) How did receiving sms reminders or being a recipient of the bolsa familia scholarship have impact on the availability of the patient for their appointment

(4) How did the condition in terms of being Hipertensive,Diabetic, handicapped or being an alcoholic affect their availability for appointment.

(5) What are the top 10 neighbourhoods in terms of both show up and absence for appointments

Findings¶

(1) For both generally defined criteria( show & absent), it was discovered from the analysis that females have the highest frequency. i.e there were more females who missed their appointments compared to males as much as there were also more females who showed up compared to males. Therefore it is fair to say that, there is a a strong correlation between gender and tendency to show up for appointments¶

(2) It appears from the analysis that there is no strong relationship between the different age groups and the availability for theor appointments.¶

(3) The analysis displayed a negative relationship the show up status of the patients and the number of sms recived and access to scholarship. This conclusion was reached because the lower the number of sms recieved and access to the scholarship scheme; the higher the patients turned up for their appointments.¶

(4) The analysis also reveals that available values for the diabetes fosters a relationship with the tendency of the patients showing up for their appointments. With the assumption that '0' reps patients that do not have diabetes while '1' means they do, we observe on the histogram that '0' is higher for 'show up', this implies Diabetes played more role in the show up rate of the patients.¶

(5) It appears from the analysis that there is no relationship between the age of the patients, the numbers of health issues suffered and their availability for the appointments¶

(6) It was observed from the cleaning of the dataset that most of the variables in each column are not really quantfiable for indepth analysis¶

(7) It was observed based on categorization that age persons carry the largest proportion of the patients¶

(8) One major limitation observed in the dataset during the course of the analysis is that the regions where the patients come from were not categorized into stratas like zones( e.g south, west, east e.t.c). The availability of these zones in the dataset would haved aided a correlation analysis between patients' tendency to show and the zones the come from. Another variable that would have helped is the distance of the hosipitals from the patients houses.¶
